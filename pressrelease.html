Stony Brook University to Host 27th Annual Fall Workshop on Computational Geometry

After being hosted by several other prestigious universities, the workshop returns to Stony Brook after almost 2 decades

Stony Brook, NY 11/22/2017 -

On December 1st through the 2nd, Stony Brook University will be hosting its 27th annual Fall Workshop on Computational Geometry. Computational Geometry is a branch of computer science dedicated to the study of algorithms that can be thought of in terms of geometry. This workshop aims to bring together researchers in the field and students in order learn about new and upcoming developments in the field of computational geometry. This workshop will be hosted by the Stony Brook University Department of Computer Science as well as the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics. The workshop will be located in the Stony Brook University New Computer Science building. It will extend over two days and it is open to the public without a registration fee. The National Science Foundation will be providing travel grants to eligible students and junior researchers that wish to attend the conference and are in need of financial support in order to attend.

In a discussion regarding the impact of this workshop, Joseph Mitchell, a Stony Brook University distinguished professor and member of the workshop’s program committee, stated “The workshop brings many different leaders of the field in one place in order to discuss the current progress that is being made in the field of computational geometry”. This workshop plays a significant role in the field of computational geometry because it’s brings together many lead researchers in order to discuss their findings and current research status. Speakers will discuss their work in their respective fields as well as the importance of computational geometry and its potential applications.

During the workshop, there will be a specific focus on open problems that exist in the computational geometry field. An open problem is a problem that can be accurately stated and is believed to have a solution that has not been discovered. An open problems session will be hosted during the workshop. This session is used to promote a free exchange of research challenges and questions in the field. A presentation on The Open Problems Project will be given in order to present new open problems and potential solutions to existing open problems. In year’s past, some of these open problems were able to be solved by researchers in the field that were present at the workshop.

Stony Brook University originally hosted the first workshop in 1991. Since then, several other distinguished universities such as MIT, Brown and UC Berkeley have taken over hosting over previous years. This year, Stony Brook is pleased to host the 27th annual Fall Workshop at Stony Brook University back where it first began. For more information regarding the workshop as well as information on how to register, please visit http://www.ams.stonybrook.edu/.
